About the Trek

Kalsubai Peak is the highest point in Maharashtra, rightfully earning its title as the 'Everest of Maharashtra'. The trek is a challenging yet rewarding journey that takes you through lush fields, rocky patches, and iron ladders. The summit offers a breathtaking 360-degree view of the surrounding Bhandardara region and several prominent forts like Alang, Madan, and Kulang. It's a must-do for any serious trekker in India, offering a true test of endurance and spirit.

Itinerary

Meet at Kasara railway station and proceed to the base village of Bari. After a quick briefing and breakfast, we begin the ascent. The trail is steep and involves navigating multiple iron ladders. We'll reach the camping spot near the summit by late afternoon. Enjoy the sunset and a campfire dinner.

Wake up early for a short climb to the Kalsubai temple at the summit to witness a spectacular sunrise. After soaking in the views, we'll descend back to the campsite for breakfast. Post breakfast, we begin our descent to the base village. Reach Bari by afternoon, have lunch, and then depart for Kasara.

Required Equipment

Clothing & Footwear
  • Trekking shoes with good grip
  • 2-3 pairs of quick-dry t-shirts
  • One warm jacket or fleece
  • Poncho or rain jacket (for monsoon)
Gear & Safety
  • A 30-40 litre backpack
  • Headlamp or torch with extra batteries
  • Personal first-aid kit
  • 2 litres water bottle & energy snacks

Trek Details

  • Start Point: Bari Village
  • Trek Distance: ~6.6 km (one way)
  • Trek Type: High-endurance, steep ascent

Getting There

The most common way is to take a local train to Kasara station from Mumbai and then hire a local jeep or taxi to Bari village, which is the base for the trek.
